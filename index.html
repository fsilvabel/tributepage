Tim Berners-Lee

The man who invented the World Wide Web


Timeline

  • 1955 - Born on in London, England
  • 1969 - Went to Emanuel School London
  • 1976 - Graduated and received a degree in physics from the Queens College, Oxford University England
  • 1976 - He spent a few years developing computer languages
  • 1978 - Worked at D.G. Nash limited where wrote typesetting software
  • 1980 - He was employed as an independent contractor at CERN in Switzerland
  • 1981 - He started a new job at John Pooles image computer systems
  • 1984 - He went back to work at CERN with a permanent position
  • 1990 - Tim produced the first version of the world wide web, the first web browser, and the first web server with the help of Robert Cailliau
  • 1990 - Tim married Nancy Carlson, an American computer programmer and former figure skater, in Connecticut
  • 1991 - The world wide web was launched on the internet making it available for the entire public world
  • 1991 - First website was built at CERN and put online on the 6th August
  • 1992 - There were over 50 web servers in the world
  • 1993 - The site was getting 10 thousand hits a day
  • 1994 - Berners-Lee founded W3C (World Wide Web consortium) at the Laboratory of Computer Science (LCS) at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology in Boston
  • 1994 - He became one of 6th members of the World Wide Web. Allowing anyone to be able to access freely
  • 2004 - He became a professor in the Computer Science Department at the University of Southampton, UK. There he worked on the Semantic Web
  • 2009 - Tim launched a charity called the World Wide Web Foundation in Uganda in 2009. This foundation goes towards the improvement and development of the world wide web
  • 2009 - He worked in a project set up by Gordon Brown to help make UK data more publicly available

"The goal of the Web is to serve humanity. We built it now so that those who come to it later will be able to create things that we cannot outselves imagine."

"The Web does not just connect machines, it connects people."
